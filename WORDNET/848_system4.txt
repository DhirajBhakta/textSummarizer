Passengers flying to and from the Indira Gandhi International (IGI) Airport here after July 7 can claim a refund of excess airport charges paid as part of their airfare, according to aviation regulator DGCA.This follows a recent tariff order slashing User Development Fee (UDF) for domestic passengers flying out of Delhi airport to Rs10 from Rs275-550.The levy was reduced to Rs45 from Rs635-1,270 for international travellers flying out of IGI.Both domestic and international passengers landing in Delhi will not have to pay the UDF anymore.Domestic passengers travelling after July 7 can claim a refund within 45 days, while international travellers have 60 days, according to the Directorate General of Civil Aviation?
