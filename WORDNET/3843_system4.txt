Bombay High Court has reduced the life sentence of a man who set his wife on fire, saying he only wanted to burn her and not kill her.According to an Indian Express report, Justice VK Tahilramani noted in his judgement that the man convicted for killing his wife, "only wanted to inflict burns and not kill her but unfortunately the situation slipped out of control to a fatal extent."
In view of the evidence on record, we are inclined to think that all that the appellant thought of was to inflict burns and not to kill her but unfortunately the situation slipped out of control to a fatal extent," Justice Tahilramani noted.The order has left people fuming.poor man -- he wanted to only burn his wife. not kill her. oops galti ho gaya, says bombay HC sigh https://t.co/H0Ze9bsInfGreat.
