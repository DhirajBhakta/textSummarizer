The top court noted that the woman might have first "unwillingly" agreed to go by her parents wish but later she apparently changed her mind which appeared from the scene of the incident where garlands, bangles and vermilion were found. 
The court noted that the victim and the accused were in love with each other and the womans father had testified in the court that due to caste differences, his family had refused to give their nod to the couple to marry. 
The mans version of the incident was held as "plausible" by the apex court which noted that as per his statement, the woman was physically abused by her family and was mercilessly beaten even on the day of incident in November 1995.
