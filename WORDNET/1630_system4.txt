Last week, the government said customers dissatisfied with service at any hotel or restaurant can opt to seek that service charge not be levied, as this is optional or discretionary as per the Consumer Protection Act.MAKE 'SERVICE CHARGE IS VOLUNTARY' NOTICE PUBLICThe Department of Consumer Affairs, in a statement, also asked state governments to advise hotels and restaurants to disseminate information, such as through displays, that "the service charges are discretionary or voluntary".The department said that it received complaints from consumers that many hotels and restaurants charged "service charge in the range of 5-20 per cent, in lieu of tips" and consumers were "forced to pay irrespective of the kind of service provided".Also read | In India, we cannot imagine food without adulteration, quips food minister Ram Vilas Paswan?
