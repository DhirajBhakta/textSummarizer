Maharashtra has become the first state to make social boycott a crime after President Pranab Mukherjee cleared the legislation. 
The Maharashtra Prohibition of People from Social Boycott (Prevention Prohibition and Redressal) Act prescribes a maximum punishment of three year in jail and a fine of Rs1lakh. 
Sudhir Shrivastava, additional chief secretary for home, told HT.The Act also provides for compensation to victims. 
The Act also has a provision to withdraw cases with the consent of victims and permission from a court. 
Hamid Dabholkar, activist of Maharashtra Andhshradha Nirmulan Samiti and son of slain anti-superstition crusader Narendra Dabholkar, said their three-and-a-half-year-old demand had been met. ?
