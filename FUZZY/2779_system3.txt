While the Indian cricket team's search for the new head coach continues following the Anil Kumble's resignation, skipper Virat Kohli on Thursday insisted that his team are currently entirely focussing on their ongoing limited-overs series against West Indies and not on anything else.Speaking on the eve of India's third ODI  against the Caribbean side at the Sir Vivian Richards Stadium, Kohli said that the appointment of the new coach is in BCCI's control and that his side's priority is to just focus on their game and win the ongoing series."
Our priority is coming here and winning this series and preparing for the game that lies ahead of us," the 28-year-old told the reporters here.When quizzed whether he has any say on the appointment of the coach, the swashbuckling batsman said that they only voice their opinion as a team and that there is no individual view involved."
