One of the most sought after all-rounders in the world at the moment Ben Stokes landed a multi-crore deal with Rising Pune Supergiants (RPS) at the Indian Premier League 2017 player auction in Bengaluru on Monday. 
(Stokes hits jackpot in historic Indian Premier League player bid)Stokes, who was recently named England's Test vice-captain, said he is having an "incredible week" and it was pretty impressive to see teams warring to buy him at the auction. 
(AUCTION HIGHLIGHTS)RPS, who made their IPL debut last season, fought off Mumbai Indians, Royal Challengers Bangalore, Delhi Daredevils and defending champions Sunrisers Hyderabad to get the 25-year-old, paying a staggering Rs 14.5 crore despite his limited availability for the Twenty20 tournament.
