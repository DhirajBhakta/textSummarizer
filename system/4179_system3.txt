With the slow progress of Delhi-Meerut expressway construction, the completion date of Prime Minister Narendra Modi's prestigious project has been further pushed back to March, 2018.National Highway Authority of India (NHAI) has divided the 90 km stretch starting from Nizamuddin bridge in the outer ring road in New Delhi to Meerut bypass into three zones. 
We have completed 15% of the construction work in segment one starting from Nizamuddin bridge along the NH 24 towards Dasna in Ghaziabad and preliminary construction of the package III started recently," the official said.The project is also aimed at expediting the improvement of infrastructure in Uttar Pradesh and in reducing the time and cost of travel, particularly for heavy traffic, plying between 'Delhi-Meerut' Uttar Pradesh Border to Dasna section on National Highway-24.The Delhi-Meerut Expressway would be an access controlled highway and 31 traffic signals have been removed from the stretch.
