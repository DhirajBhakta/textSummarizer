In a hearing on its decision to impose demonetisation of Rs 500 and Rs 1000 currency notes, the government today told the Supreme Court that the problems being faced by people will be over in "10-15 days".Attorney General Mukul Rohatgi, representing the government, said it is "not sitting around doing nothing" and claimed no violence or unrest has been reported in the last month."
The apex court is hearing a plea seeking rationale behind putting the entire nation to "such great difficulties".The Supreme Court asked, "When you made the policy on demonetisation, was it confidential?" 
The three-judge bench has framed nine questions for the adjudication to decide whether demonetisation was unconstitutional or not.NO CASH IN BANKS, ATMs: PETITIONERS Appearing on behalf of the petitioner, noted lawyer Prashant Bhusan argued that there was no preparation fro m the government to deal with the impending situation caused by demonetisation."
