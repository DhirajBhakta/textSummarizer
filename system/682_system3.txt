Venezuelas opposition have announced a two-day national strike against President Nicolas Maduro following a day of violent clashes in Caracas on Saturday where the injured included a violinist who has become the face of the protests.Neither rubber bullets nor pellets will stop our fight for Venezuelas independence, said musician Wuilly Arteaga. 
Maduro calls himself a flag bearer for the international left, up against right-wing terrorists seeking a coup with the connivance of the US and the international media.The opposition is stepping up street tactics in what it dubs zero hour for Venezuela to try to block the new Constitutional Assembly Maduro wants to set up.
