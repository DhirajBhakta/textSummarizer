In 2015, he set up an improvised laboratory in Ouagadougou to industrialise the production of shea caterpillars  insects that feed on the leaves of shea trees.Traditionally eaten in many parts of Burkina Faso, caterpillars are now sold at markets throughout the country as a tasty treat. 
Armed with the prize and his degree, Hien worked on developing his business for two years before finally selling his first caterpillars to market traders.It didnt start off well, he recalls.
