Former Bharatiya Janata Party MP and cricketer-turned politician, Navjot Singh Sidhu on Saturday won the Amritsar (East) Assembly election in Punjab.He defeated his nearest rival, Rajesh Honey of the BJP, with a margin of 42,661 votes. 
Aam Aadmi Party's Sarabjot Singh came in third, polling 14604 votes.A three-time MP from the Amritsar Lok Sabha seat, Sidhu contested his maiden assembly election this time after joining the Congress.His rivals too are first timers. 
Amritsar East constituency was once of the most watched constituency this time as Sidhu not only made the contest triangular but also faced BJP, which had a stronghold in Amritsar district.
