The eight-year-old had become the first child in the world to receive two new hands in a procedure that seemed to herald a revolution in transplant medicine.Two years on, the sports-mad boy from Baltimore, Maryland, is enjoying the freedom and independence his new hands have given him. 
As of 18 months after transplantation surgery he is able to write and feed, toilet and dress himself more independently and efficiently than he could do before transplantation, writes the team from the Childrens hospital of Philadelphia in the Lancet Child and Adolescent Health.Organ transplantation is risky in that a recipients body may reject the new body part, while the drug regime involved carries a series of health risks.
