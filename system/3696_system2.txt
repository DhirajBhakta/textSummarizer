Twenty-one dowry deaths are reported across the country every day, but the conviction rate is only 34.7 per cent.The National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) states that in 2015, as many as 7,634 women died in the country due to dowry harassment. 
Improper investigations at the initial stage of a case slow down the process of judicial proceedings, experts rue.Items such as jewellery, clothes, cars and money are traditionally given by the bride's family to the groom and his parents under the outlawed custom to ensure that she is taken care of in her new home.
