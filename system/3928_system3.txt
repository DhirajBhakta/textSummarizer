2 lakh to settle insider trading case.
New Delhi, Apr 5 (PTI) Bharti Airtels promoter firm Indian Continent Investment has settled an alleged insider trading case after paying Rs 2.17 lakh towards settlement charges to regulator Sebi. 
Indian Continent Investment had acquired 26,58,60,986 shares constituting 7 per cent stake in the company during July 2007 to December 2010. 
As a result, Indian Continent Investments shareholding in Bharti Aitel crossed 5 per cent on April 29, 2008, requiring it to make necessary disclosures under PIT (Prohibition of Insider Trading) Regulations. 
However, it was alleged that the promoter company failed to make such disclosure, prompting Sebi to initiate a probe. 
In a settlement order passed today, Sebi said it is disposing of "the adjudication proceedings initiated against the noticee (Indian Continent Investment).
